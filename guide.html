    • Coding Helper Applications Guide

    A "Coding Helper" is a staff position within our server that demonstrates your profound understanding of programming.
    If accepted, you are expected to volunteer your time to help the community members solve their programming issues.

    What are our requirements?

  • Activity. This is our number one requirement as we need to complete all the help requests we receive.
  • Maturity. Part of being on our staff team is having a mature attitude. You MUST show maturity within our server to be a helper.
  • Proficiency. To successfuly help our members, we require that you have an advanced understanding of a programming language.
  • Respectfulness. As the face of the community, you must show respect to all members while helping & interacting them. This includes admins/mods & other helpers.

    • These are the minimum standards we look for in the coding helper applications we receive.
    With that in mind, its best practice to ensure that you have met these standards before applying. If you have any requirement questions, don't hesitate to DM @modmail on Discord!

    How do we review apps?

    We understand that knowing the reviewal process for applications such as this allow you to better your chances at getting accepted
    With that in mind, here are all of the steps we take to review your coding helper application, including time, process, and more.

    Expected Time: we operate our application reviews on a monthly cycle. First we accept applications, then the new helpers become trial helpers.
    All trial helpers typically have a minimum of 20-30 days of their trial period before they can become actual coding helpers. During this time,
    the trial helpers show how they help, how active they are, and more. We DO NOT review applications during current trial periods. This
    Once the trial period is over, we complete promotions and demotions of the trial helpers based on their trial period. Following demotions, our
    next cycle of applications is then reviewed by admins. In total, you should expect to hear back from us within 30 days of submitting your app.

    Review Process: now that we are reviewing your application, there are some steps we take to make our decision. First, we read your application,
    we look at all of your responses, and take in to consideration the programming language you want to help with. After getting an understanding of
    who you are, and the general information about you, we take a look at your messages within our server. We look for the standards set above,
    this means we are looking for how you act within the server- respectfulness, activity, proficiency, maturity, and how much you currently help members.
    After taking all this information into account, the administration team makes their decision to either accept you into your trial phase, or deny your app.

    How We Respond: after your application is reviewed, you will receive your results via email. The email you provide in the application is the email
    that we will send the results to; providing a fake email will result in your results not being received. If you are denied, you will receive a basic reason of why,
    however, we will not go into detail on your full application results. If you get accepted, you will receive more information on your trial phase, and your responsibilities
    moving forward.

    Having patience is very important while we review your application. Applicants that show signs of impatience such as asking about the status of their app, constantly bringing it up,
    or even getting annoyed about the lack of response are typically denied. Maintaining all of the requirements and having patience during the reviewal time is the best way to get accepted.

    Extra Information

  • Will it help to DM @modmail regarding my application? Yes and no. DMing @modmail with questions to better your application is usually a good thing,
    and we will be happy to answer them. However, if you are DMing @modmail for your application's status, your ticket will be closed as we cannot provide
    an exact time that you will hear back from us.

  • We mostly look for activity in our coding helper applications. Ideally, applicants should be active and helping members at least once a day.
    furthermore, maintaining this level of activity during your application process is ideal if you want to get accepted. Most members
    get accepted, and then go inactive during the trial phase- be sure to maintain activity even after being accepted.

    • If you have any followup questions or concerns, feel free to contact @modmail on discord.
